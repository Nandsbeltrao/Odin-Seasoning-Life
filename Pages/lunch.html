Ingredients

  • 4 salmon fillets (approximately 180-200 g each)
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon (or 2 tablespoons)
  • 2 garlic cloves, chopped or minced
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon of dried herbs (such as dill, thyme, or rosemary) or fresh chopped herbs (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon of Dijon mustard (optional, for an extra flavor boost)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Marinade: In a small bowl, mix together the olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, salt, black pepper, and dried or fresh herbs (if using). If desired, add Dijon mustard for an extra touch.
  2. Season the Salmon: Place the salmon fillets in a dish or shallow plate and brush them with the marinade. Let them marinate for at least 15 minutes to absorb the flavors. If possible, let them marinate in the refrigerator for up to 1 hour.
  3. Preheat the Grill: Heat a grill or non-stick skillet over medium-high heat. If using a grill, make sure it is well-heated before adding the salmon.
  4. Grill the Salmon: Place the salmon fillets on the hot grill or skillet, skin side down. Grill for about 4-5 minutes on each side, depending on the thickness of the fillets. The salmon should release easily from the grill when it is ready to flip.
  5. Check for Doneness: The salmon is done when the flesh turns opaque and flakes easily with a fork. Avoid overcooking to prevent it from drying out.
  6. Serve: Transfer the salmon fillets to a plate and let them rest for a few minutes before serving.
  7. Accompaniments: Serve the grilled salmon with a fresh salad, roasted vegetables, rice, or quinoa.

Tips ;)

  • If you want to add an extra touch, squeeze a bit of fresh lemon over the salmon before serving.
