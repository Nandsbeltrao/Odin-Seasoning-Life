Ingredients

  • 200 g of semi-sweet chocolate (or milk chocolate, if you prefer
  • 3 eggs
  • 1/4 cup of sugar
  • 1/2 cup of heavy cream (or 1 can of cream)
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract (optional)
  • A pinch of salt
  • Chocolate shavings or cocoa powder for garnish (optional)

Steps

  1. Melt the Chocolate: Break the chocolate into pieces and place it in a heatproof bowl. Melt the chocolate using a double boiler: place the bowl over a pot of simmering water (ensuring the water does not touch the bowl) and stir until the chocolate is completely melted and smooth. Alternatively, you can melt the chocolate in the microwave in 30-second intervals, stirring between intervals.
  2. Prepare the Egg Whites: Separate the egg whites from the yolks. Place the egg whites in a large bowl and add a pinch of salt. Beat the egg whites until they form stiff peaks. Using an electric mixer can make this process easier.
  3. Mix the Egg Yolks and Chocolate: In another bowl, beat the egg yolks with the sugar until they are light and frothy. Add the melted chocolate (cooled to warm, not hot) and the vanilla extract, and mix well.
  4. Fold in the Heavy Cream: Add the fresh cream to the chocolate and egg yolk mixture, stirring gently until well combined.
  5. Add the Egg Whites: Gently fold the beaten egg whites into the chocolate mixture. Use a spatula and make smooth, upward motions to preserve the airy texture of the mousse.
  6. Refrigerate: Transfer the mousse to individual glasses or a large bowl. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or until the mousse is well set.
  7. Garnish and Serve: Before serving, you can decorate with chocolate shavings, cocoa powder, or a bit of whipped cream, if desired./li>

Tips ;)

  • For a special touch, you can add a bit of orange liqueur or rum to the chocolate mixture.
